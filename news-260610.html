Pattaya Arsenal Case Expands: Chinese Suspect Sun Mingchen Linked to Transnational Scam Syndicates, Thai Navy Officers Charged with Illegal Weapons Trafficking

A routine traffic accident in Chon Buri province has unravelled one of Thailand's most serious national security cases in recent years, leading to the arrest of 31-year-old Chinese national Sun Mingchen and the subsequent detention of at least 11 additional suspects across seven interconnected criminal investigations involving weapons trafficking, cyberscam networks, money laundering, and fraudulent procurement of official Thai identity documents.

The chain of events began on 8 May, when police were alerted to a vehicle rollover accident in Bang Lamung district, Chon Buri. A subsequent search of Sun's rented residence near Pattaya revealed a cache of military-grade weapons, including M16 and M4 assault rifles, a handgun, fragmentation grenades, C4 explosives, bulletproof vests, and four containers of fuel.

Sun was charged with possessing firearms and explosives without permission. He reportedly confessed to all charges and claimed he had been suffering from depression and intended the weapons for a suicide bombing, though psychiatric evaluation was ordered to verify this claim. Further investigation revealed that Sun held both Chinese and Cambodian passports, as well as a pink identification card for long-term foreign residents. He had re-entered Thailand on a multi-year visa permit.

The probe expanded rapidly. Five Thai suspects — including navy officers — were charged with the illegal possession and sale of weapons of war after investigators traced the arms supply chain back to a Pattaya shooting range. All denied the charges and stated they would prove their innocence in court.

Police also uncovered strong financial links. Authorities confirmed Sun acted as a financial wallet for scam syndicates based in neighbouring countries, particularly Cambodia, with international money transfers traced through connected accounts. A separate sub-case involved the fraudulent application for Sun's pink ID card, which led to the arrest of a former district official.

The case prompted the Prime Minister to summon senior police officers for urgent discussions and has led to calls for stricter immigration screening and a review of long-term visa privileges. Police said the investigation reflects broader vulnerabilities in Thailand's status as an international hub, and that surveillance operations had been expanded to tourist destinations including Phuket and Hua Hin.
