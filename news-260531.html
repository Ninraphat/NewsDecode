Thai Court Acquits Thanathorn on Royal Defamation Charges

A criminal court in Thailand on Thursday acquitted the popular leader of a progressive political movement on charges of defaming the king and violating the law on online activity.

Thanathorn Juangroongruangkit was charged based on comments he made during a Facebook Live broadcast in 2021 about the awarding of a government COVID-19 vaccine production contract to a company owned by King Maha Vajiralongkorn. His comments criticised that the government bungled its vaccination campaign.

A press release from the Bangkok Criminal Court said comments did not contain any malicious or defamatory message towards the king. Thailand's lese-majeste law carries a prison term of up to 15 years for insulting the monarchy, but critics say it is often wielded as a tool to quash political dissent. The Computer Crime Act charge is punishable by five years.

It is rare to see an acquittal in a royal defamation case. Thailand's machinery of state remains deeply conservative. Its political establishment is sensitive about threats to the monarchy.

"Personally, I feel relieved," Thanathorn said. He called for the rights of political prisoners to be respected.

Thanathorn's party was dissolved by a court order in 2024. Its latest incarnation, the People's Party, is now the main opposition party.

His prosecution was condemned by human rights groups. A verdict of not guilty was broadly welcomed as a rare moment of judicial independence.
