Off-Duty Border Patrol Officer Shoots Dead Two Motorcycle Taxi Riders and Wounds Third After Fare Dispute in Bangkok; Later Granted Bail with Strict Conditions

An off-duty Border Patrol Police officer shot dead two motorcycle taxi riders and wounded a third in the early hours of 14 June 2026, following a heated argument over a motorcycle taxi fare in the Huai Khwang area of Bangkok. The suspect, Police Lance Corporal Namthap Pakwattana, 33, subsequently surrendered to authorities at Makkasan Police Station and handed over his service weapon — a 9mm Sig Sauer pistol — which was seized as evidence.

According to police, the incident occurred at approximately 2:50 am on Soi Prachasongkhro 38 near Huai Khwang Market in the Ratchada area of Din Daeng district. Namthap had travelled from Surin province to Bangkok to sit a career promotion examination and had earlier met his girlfriend at a nearby restaurant before accompanying her to public transport. He then approached a group of motorcycle taxi riders to arrange a ride to his accommodation in the Makkasan area. A rider quoted a fare of 120 baht, which Namthap reportedly considered excessive and asked to be reduced by 60 baht, triggering a verbal altercation that rapidly escalated into a physical confrontation.

CCTV footage and witness testimony showed that motorcycle taxi rider Charin Khunarak, 48, kicked the officer, causing him to fall to the ground, before another rider, Phurit Mangthisan, 37, also kicked him. Namthap then allegedly drew his firearm and fired multiple shots at the group. Phurit was found face up at the scene with bullet wounds to his back and exit wounds through his chest; he was declared dead at the scene. Charin sustained gunshot wounds to his thigh and hand, was transported to hospital, but later succumbed to his injuries. A third rider, Kiatisak Srirat, 46, was struck once in the thigh and survived. Witnesses told Channel 7 that Namthap appeared to initiate the confrontation and may have been under the influence of alcohol, though police did not publicly confirm this.

On 15 June, investigators escorted Namthap to the Criminal Court for a remand hearing. He was formally charged with intentional murder, attempted murder, and carrying a firearm in a public place without permission. Police requested a 12-day detention period to gather further evidence. During the transfer to court, Namthap kept his head down and wore a hat to conceal his face. When approached by reporters, he offered only a brief apology and claimed the victims had started the fight.

The deceased riders’ wives arrived at court to oppose the bail application, urging the judge to deny release on the grounds that Namthap might flee or tamper with witnesses. The accused's father, a retired police officer, offered a luxury condominium valued at one million baht as security for his son’s release. The court ultimately granted bail of 500,000 baht, but imposed strict conditions: Namthap is prohibited from contacting the victims’ wives or relatives, leaving Thailand, carrying any firearm, or interfering with witnesses in the neighbourhood. Breach of any condition would result in immediate cancellation of his bail.

Relatives of the suspect said that Namthap had a mental health condition and was taking prescribed medication, but attributed the shooting to a momentary loss of temper rather than his condition. The case has sparked intense public debate about the use of lethal force by off-duty officers, police accountability, and special privileges that might be extended to law enforcement personnel during legal proceedings.
