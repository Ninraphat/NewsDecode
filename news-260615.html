Off-Duty Border Patrol Police Officer Shoots Dead Two Motorcycle Taxi Riders and Wounds Third After Fare Dispute in Bangkok; Later Granted Bail with Strict Bail Conditions

An off-duty Border Patrol Police officer shot dead two motorcycle taxi riders and wounded a third in the early hours of 14 June 2026, following a heated argument over a motorcycle taxi fare in the Huai Khwang area of Bangkok. The suspect, Police Lance Corporal Namthap Pakwattana, 33, subsequently surrendered to authorities at Makkasan Police Station and handed over his service weapon — a 9mm Sig Sauer pistol — which was seized as evidence.

According to police, the incident occurred at approximately 2:50 am on Soi Prachasongkhro 38 near Huai Khwang Market in Din Daeng district. Namthap had travelled from Surin province to Bangkok to sit a career promotion examination and had earlier met his girlfriend at a nearby restaurant. He then approached a group of motorcycle taxi riders to arrange a ride. A rider quoted a fare of 120 baht, which Namthap considered excessive and asked to be reduced by 60 baht, triggering a verbal altercation that rapidly escalated into a physical confrontation.

CCTV footage and witness testimony showed that rider Charin Khunarak, 48, kicked the officer, causing him to fall to the ground, before another rider also kicked him. Namthap then allegedly drew his firearm and fired multiple shots at the group. Phurit Mangthisan, 37, was found at the scene with bullet wounds to his back and exit wounds through his chest; he was declared dead at the scene. Charin sustained gunshot wounds and was transported to hospital, but later succumbed to his injuries. A third rider, Kiatisak Srirat, 46, was struck once in the thigh and survived. Witnesses said Namthap may have been under the influence of alcohol, though police did not publicly confirm this.

On 15 June, investigators escorted Namthap to the Criminal Court for a remand hearing. He was formally charged with intentional murder, attempted murder, and carrying a firearm in a public place. Police requested a 12-day detention period to gather further evidence. During the transfer to court, Namthap wore a hat to conceal his face. When approached by reporters, he offered only a brief apology and claimed the victims had started the fight.

The deceased riders' wives arrived at court to oppose bail, urging the judge to deny release on the grounds that Namthap might flee or tamper with witnesses. The accused's father, a retired police officer, offered a luxury condominium as security. The court ultimately granted bail of 500,000 baht, but imposed strict bail conditions: Namthap is prohibited from contacting the victims' relatives, leaving Thailand, carrying any firearm, or interfering with witnesses. The case has sparked intense debate about the use of lethal force by off-duty officers, police accountability, and special privileges extended to law enforcement during legal proceedings.

Relatives of the suspect said Namthap had a mental health condition and was taking prescribed medication, but attributed the shooting to a momentary loss of temper. They denied that his condition was responsible for the incident.
