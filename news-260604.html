Police Intensify Hunt for Killer of Seven-Year-Old Girl Found in Kanchanaburi Rubber Plantation

Thai police have stepped up their investigation into the murder of a seven-year-old girl, known as "Nong Angoon" (real name Kasama), whose body was discovered in a rubber plantation in Sangkhla Buri district, Kanchanaburi province on Monday — two days after she vanished from her village.

The girl was last seen on Saturday afternoon when she left her home in Ban Thi Khrong, tambon Prang Phle, to buy sweets at a nearby grocery store. A shop owner confirmed she had purchased 30 baht's worth of candies before she disappeared. A latex collector found her body the following Monday morning, lying face down among rubber trees approximately 200 metres from Highway 323. She was still wearing a black T-shirt and pink pants.

Preliminary examinations revealed signs of physical assault, including two distinct wounds beneath her chin and severe bruising across her face and neck. Forensic investigators immediately secured the crime scene and transferred her body to the Institute of Forensic Medicine in Bangkok for a full autopsy to determine the exact cause of death.

Pol Gen Thana Choowong, deputy national police chief, personally flew in to Kanchanaburi to oversee the investigation on Tuesday. He instructed investigators and forensic officers to meticulously gather all available evidence and ensure the case was handled with the utmost urgency.

Forensic teams collected fingerprints and DNA samples from three Buddhist monks residing at a nearby monastery, as well as from a local pickup truck driver, all of whom were considered persons of interest. Police emphasised that these biometric tests were routine procedures to either rule out or confirm suspect involvement. All cooperated fully with the testing.

Officers also detained an undocumented worker for questioning, though no formal charges have yet been filed. Authorities are awaiting the autopsy results before making any arrests.

The case has sparked widespread public outrage and grief across Thailand, drawing attention to the vulnerability of children in remote communities. Senior officers vowed to apprehend the perpetrator as quickly as possible.
