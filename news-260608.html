Police Conclude Channel 8 Reporter Nattawut Ponglangka Died by Suicide After Ingesting Potassium Cyanide; One Man Charged with Illegal Possession of Hazardous Substance

Thai police have concluded that Nattawut "Nass" Ponglangka, 35, a well-known field reporter and news anchor at Channel 8, died by suicide after ingesting potassium cyanide at his townhouse in Bang Kruai district, Nonthaburi, on the morning of 30 November 2025. The case, which had gripped Thailand for weeks amid widespread speculation over whether the death was suicide or homicide, was formally resolved following an extensive police investigation.

Nattawut was initially found dead on his living room floor by friends who had been with him the night before. His death was originally attributed to sudden unexplained nocturnal death syndrome, locally known as lai tai, a condition associated with unexpected death during sleep. However, public suspicion grew rapidly after social media users raised the possibility of poisoning, and the cremation was halted when a government official publicly called for further investigation.

On 6 December 2025, Thailand's Central Institute of Forensic Science released a toxicology report confirming lethal levels of cyanide in both his bloodstream and stomach, sufficient to cause rapid death. Police immediately launched a formal investigation, summoning five close associates who had been present or in contact with Nattawut around the time of his death.

CCTV footage from inside the property proved decisive. It showed Nattawut retrieving a cyanide packet from a computer desk at 6:19 am. Approximately 40 seconds later, he returned the packet to the same location. No other person was seen entering the room during that period, ruling out third-party involvement.

A man identified only as Kitti subsequently admitted to police that he had supplied cyanide to Nattawut on multiple occasions from October, having obtained the substance from a gold shop where he worked. A separate colleague, Sarawat "Ice" Kijpanit, a fellow Channel 8 news anchor, surrendered a package of cyanide to investigators, stating he had retrieved it from Nattawut's home. Chat logs showing Nattawut discussing the procurement of cyanide were also handed to police by his supervisor, Phutta Apiwan.

Dep Comm Pol Maj Gen Atthapol Anusit confirmed that investigators had concluded the death was self-inflicted. Kitti was formally charged with illegal possession of a Type-3 hazardous substance under Thai law. Police said the case would also serve as a benchmark for improving crime scene investigation procedures in Thailand, noting that the initial misclassification of the death had complicated the inquiry.

Nattawut had been the recipient of the Thep Narai Nakarat Award for Outstanding Field Reporter in 2025. Friends who testified described him as appearing cheerful and enthusiastic about future plans on the night before his death, with no signs of suicidal ideation. The case drew national attention not only for its tragic outcome but also for raising questions about occupational stress among journalists and the adequacy of initial death classification procedures.
