Rock Star Sek Loso Released on Special Parole with Electronic Monitoring After 13 Months in Prison

Thailand's celebrated rock musician Seksan Sookpimay, widely known by his stage name "Sek Loso", was released from the Khao Kling prison camp in Kaeng Krachan district, Phetchaburi province, on 24 June 2026, after serving 13 months of a nearly three-year sentence for weapons and drug offences. His release followed a grant of special parole approved by the Department of Corrections and the Justice Minister, and he was fitted with an electronic monitoring device upon discharge. A large crowd of fans and media gathered outside the facility to welcome the parolee.

The case against Sek Loso originated in late December 2017, when he was accused of firing shots into the air at a temple in Nakhon Si Thammarat. Police obtained an arrest warrant, and he was later apprehended at his Bangkok home, where authorities alleged he resisted officers and was found in possession of an unlicensed firearm and ammunition. He also admitted to drug use. After a lengthy series of appeals, the Supreme Court on 20 May 2025 upheld his conviction on all three charges — illegal possession of a firearm, resisting officers in the course of duty, and drug use — and imposed a total sentence of two years, 12 months and 20 days without suspension.

On 3 June 2026, a Royal Decree on pardons came into effect, reducing Seksan's remaining sentence by five months and 20 days. His revised total sentence became two years, six months and 30 days, now set to expire on 13 December 2027. While incarcerated, the rock star participated in a special rehabilitation programme under the "Inspiration Project" — a vocational and life-skills initiative held at Khao Kling under the royal patronage of Her Royal Highness Princess Bajrakitiyabha. The programme applied the sufficiency economy philosophy to help inmates reintegrate into society. Seksan demonstrated good conduct and met all evaluation criteria throughout the five-month training.

The subcommittee on sentence suspension recommended special parole for Seksan at its fifth meeting on 27 May 2026. The Justice Minister formally approved the parole on 19 June 2026, and Phetchaburi Central Prison proceeded with his discharge on 24 June. During the probation period, which runs until the completion of his original sentence in December 2027, Seksan must comply with all conditions set by probation officers and wear the electronic monitoring bracelet at all times. Failure to adhere to these conditions could result in revocation of his parole.

The release attracted widespread media attention and sparked debate about consistency in how Thai courts and corrections authorities handle celebrity inmates. On his Facebook page, Seksan wrote: "Today is a day of freedom. The waiting is over. The test has become a lesson, and today marks a new beginning." Supporters, including prominent political figure Nattawut Saikuar, were present at the facility to greet him. Critics, however, questioned whether the parole system applied equally across all offenders, regardless of public profile or social connections. The case remains one of the most closely watched criminal proceedings involving a Thai public figure in recent years.
