Bolt Permanently Bans Driver Who Assaulted Japanese Restaurateur in Bangkok Fare Dispute; Government Orders Platform to Explain

Ride-hailing platform Bolt Thailand has permanently banned and terminated the driver accused of assaulting a 52-year-old Japanese restaurateur in the Asok area of Bangkok on 28 May, following a dispute over a previously agreed fare. The company announced the action on Wednesday after the incident went viral on social media and triggered a wave of public outrage and government intervention.

The complainant, who owns a Japanese restaurant in Bangkok, said he had booked a ride through the Bolt app to travel from his home to work. The driver abruptly stopped the vehicle near Sukhumvit Road and told him: "For this fare, I can only go this far," before ordering him to exit the vehicle. When the passenger objected, the driver allegedly punched him repeatedly, leaving him with a head wound and multiple bruises. Witnesses at the scene helped the victim travel to Thong Lor Police Station, where he filed a formal complaint.

The incident was not immediately publicised but gained traction after the Facebook page "Jmoi V+" shared details on Monday, causing the story to spread rapidly across Thai and international media. Bolt Thailand confirmed the driver had been using his own registered account, contrary to initial speculation that he may have been impersonating another driver.

The case attracted senior government attention. Prime Minister's Office Minister Supamas Isarabhakdi intervened directly, ordering Bolt to provide a full public explanation by 5 June. She emphasised that the attack was a clear violation of the Consumer Protection Act of 1979, which guarantees equal safety rights to both Thai citizens and foreign nationals. Under the law, service providers are prohibited from abandoning or harming paying customers under any circumstances.

Authorities also announced that ride-hailing companies would be summoned to a mandatory meeting on 12 June to review driver licensing, vetting procedures, and passenger safety standards across all platforms operating in Thailand. Officials said the case had exposed systemic weaknesses in how companies monitor driver behaviour and handle passenger complaints.

Bolt stated the driver had expressed willingness to apologise and take responsibility for his actions but respected the victim's decision not to meet him. The platform said it was fully cooperating with police and government investigators.

The case has reignited concerns about tourist safety in Bangkok, following other recent high-profile incidents involving foreign visitors, and has placed fresh pressure on ride-hailing platforms to strengthen their accountability mechanisms.
