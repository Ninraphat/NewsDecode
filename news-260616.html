Arrest Warrant Issued for Man Accused of Repeatedly Sexually Assaulting 13-Year-Old Girl in Buri Ram for Nearly Two Years; Grandmother Accused of Covering Up Abuse

A 47-year-old man has been accused of repeatedly sexually assaulting a 13-year-old girl in Buri Ram province over a period of nearly two years, and police have obtained an arrest warrant after the victim finally disclosed the abuse to her paternal grandfather earlier this month. The case has also led to allegations that the girl's maternal grandmother actively concealed the ongoing abuse, allegations that authorities are now separately investigating.

The victim, a Mathayom 2 (Grade 8) student whose identity is withheld to protect her privacy, was living with her maternal grandmother and the woman's partner following the death of her great-grandfather. Family members allege that the grandmother's partner repeatedly assaulted the girl and threatened her into silence over the course of the abuse. Relatives further alleged that the grandmother arranged for the girl to receive a contraceptive implant in order to prevent pregnancy and hide the assaults. Those claims have not been independently verified.

The girl reportedly confided in her paternal grandfather on 1 June, and her aunt accompanied her to file a complaint at Krasang Police Station the following day. After the complaint was lodged, the suspect allegedly visited the grandfather's home on two consecutive nights, attempting to take the girl back and intimidate her relatives. When reporters visited the property where the abuse allegedly took place, it was found locked and appeared deserted. Villagers reported that the suspect and the grandmother had sold their cattle and fled the area.

Buri Ram provincial police confirmed they had successfully obtained an official arrest warrant and were actively tracking the suspect. Community advocates and local MPs called on police to ensure the family receives around-the-clock protection until the suspect is safely in custody. Social workers from the Buri Ram Provincial Office of Social Development and Human Security have stepped in to provide initial assistance and mental health counselling to help the young girl process the trauma.

The case has prompted calls from child protection advocates for stricter enforcement of laws protecting minors who are vulnerable to abuse within their own households, particularly in cases where the abuser holds authority over the child through a family relationship. Advocates noted that the prolonged duration of the alleged abuse — and the failure of adults in the household to report it — raised serious concerns about the adequacy of existing safeguarding mechanisms for children living in non-standard family arrangements.
