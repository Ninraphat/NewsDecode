Shocking Twist in Nong Angoon Case: 13-Year-Old Stepsister and 17-Year-Old Uncle Arrested for Suffocating and Concealing Body of Seven-Year-Old Kanchanaburi Girl

A week-long investigation into the death of seven-year-old Kasama, widely known as "Nong Angoon," took a devastating and unexpected turn when police arrested the victim's own 13-year-old stepsister, Kanda, and her 17-year-old uncle, Pae, after both allegedly confessed to their roles in causing the child's death and concealing her body.

Police said the incident occurred on the afternoon of 30 May, when the two girls were playing together at home. According to Kanda's statement to investigators, an argument broke out over a shared toy and mobile phone. During the altercation, Kanda allegedly covered the younger girl's mouth and nose, causing her to suffocate. After realising what had happened, Kanda panicked and summoned her uncle Pae to help. Together, the two juveniles concealed the body in a wardrobe inside the house.

Several hours later, they transferred the body to a motorcycle fitted with a sidecar and drove to a rubber plantation near a shrine, where they disposed of the body beneath a bamboo grove before returning home and behaving as if nothing had occurred.

Police confirmed the arrests and said the 17-year-old had been escorted to re-enact the crime at two locations — the victim's home and the disposal site. The autopsy confirmed the cause of death as respiratory failure and circulatory failure, consistent with suffocation. Earlier preliminary examinations had indicated possible signs of sexual assault, but investigators have not yet formally confirmed this allegation.

The revelation that two juvenile family members were responsible came as a profound shock. The victim's father questioned whether a dispute over a toy or phone could truly have led to his daughter's death. The mother was in severe distress and was receiving mental health counselling.

Both suspects are juveniles under the supervision of the Kanchanaburi Juvenile and Family Court. Under Thai law, minors must be processed through the juvenile justice system and cannot be tried as adults. Investigators said the case would continue as they sought to establish the full sequence of events and determine whether additional charges were warranted. The investigation had earlier included police oversight from senior officers and had involved questioning over 40 individuals and collecting more than 25 DNA samples.
