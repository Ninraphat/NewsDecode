Chinese Fugitive Behind Massive Pyramid Scheme Apprehended in Samut Prakan Luxury Home; ACE Platform Defrauded Nearly 470,000 Victims of Over 10 Billion Baht

Officers from the Crime Suppression Division (CSD), working in close coordination with Interpol and the Immigration Police, have apprehended a 39-year-old Chinese fugitive who had been in hiding at a luxury residence in Samut Prakan province, wanted in connection with running one of Thailand's largest online pyramid schemes. The suspect had been operating a fraudulent investment platform known as ACE, which deceived nearly 470,000 victims and illegally processed transactions worth more than 10 billion baht.

According to police, the ACE platform was designed to mimic a legitimate cryptocurrency and forex trading service, luring victims with promises of high returns on their investments. The platform was marketed aggressively through social media and word-of-mouth recruitment, a tactic common to multilevel pyramid structures that rely on new participants paying existing ones rather than generating real financial returns. Once the platform had accumulated sufficient funds, it collapsed, leaving the vast majority of investors with nothing.

The suspect had evaded Thai authorities for an extended period by moving between locations and keeping a low profile in the Samut Prakan area. Investigators received intelligence pointing to his whereabouts through coordinated efforts with Interpol, which had issued a Red Notice against him. Officers from the CSD then moved in with immigration officials to execute the arrest at the luxury property.

Seized from the suspect at the time of arrest were electronic devices, financial records, and documentation linked to the ACE platform's operations. Police said the evidence collected will be used to build a comprehensive case against him under Thailand's Computer Crime Act, anti-money laundering laws, and relevant securities fraud statutes. The suspect was transferred to CSD headquarters for formal interrogation and will be prosecuted in coordination with the Department of Special Investigation (DSI), which has jurisdiction over large-scale financial crime cases.

The arrest comes amid an intensifying crackdown by Thai authorities on transnational online fraud networks, particularly those targeting Thai and foreign investors with bogus cryptocurrency and forex schemes. Officials noted that the scale of the ACE fraud — affecting nearly half a million people — made it one of the most significant financial crime cases in Thailand's recent history, and they vowed to continue pursuing all co-conspirators, whether based in Thailand or abroad.
