Ex-Convict Arrested After Allegedly Sexually Assaulting and Murdering 12-Year-Old Stepdaughter in Nong Bua Lamphu

Police in Si Bun Rueang district, Nong Bua Lamphu province, apprehended a 38-year-old man identified as Pornchai on 25 May following the alleged sexual assault and murder of his 12-year-old stepdaughter, known as Amy, and a knife attack on his 33-year-old wife, On-uma. The suspect was found to be under the influence of narcotics at the time of his arrest and reportedly smiled at officers during the apprehension.

Officers were dispatched to a residence in the district after Amy's grandfather discovered the scene. Investigators found Amy's body outside a bathroom at the rear of the property. Both of her arms and legs had been bound with rope, and a cloth and rope were wrapped around her neck. Multiple knife wounds were found across her body. Investigators suspect the child was sexually assaulted prior to being killed. Her body was transferred to Loei Hospital for a full autopsy.

During interrogation, Pornchai confessed to the crimes. He told police that during the village's traditional Bun Bang Fai rocket festival, he observed a man speaking with his wife and stepdaughter and became intensely jealous. He subsequently forced both victims back to the house, then consumed a methamphetamine pill and, under what investigators described as a drug-induced psychosis, carried out the assault and killed the girl. He concealed the child's body in a storage room before fleeing.

Police confirmed through a urine test that narcotics were present in Pornchai's system. He had a prior criminal record, having been previously convicted and imprisoned for drug offences and the sexual assault of an elderly woman in the same district. Following his release from prison, he lived with his parents before entering into a relationship with On-uma.

Thai media portrayed Amy as a diligent student with plans to study abroad. The case has drawn significant public attention and renewed calls for stricter monitoring of ex-convicts and tougher drug enforcement in rural communities.
