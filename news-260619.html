A 16-year-old boy has been arrested in Rayong province after allegedly stabbing his girlfriend to death and concealing her body inside a black rubbish bag, which he then disposed of in a public rubbish bin at the roadside in an attempt to hide the crime. The case has been referred to the juvenile justice system, and formal charges have not yet been filed pending the outcome of a detailed post-mortem examination.

According to investigators, the incident occurred after the suspect became enraged upon seeing the victim communicating online with another man while the two were together. The dispute stemmed from jealousy and what the boy described as personal relationship issues. He allegedly stabbed the victim twice in the abdomen, causing her death. He then placed her body inside a black rubbish bag, tied it closed using a hooded long-sleeved shirt, and disposed of it in a roadside bin in an attempt to conceal the crime.

During police questioning, the teenager allegedly admitted responsibility for the killing, citing jealousy and relationship issues as his motive. He is currently in police custody and is being processed under juvenile justice procedures, as Thai law does not permit minors to be tried as adults. The case will be handled by the Juvenile and Family Court, which has jurisdiction over criminal cases involving individuals under the age of 18.

Officers from the local police station are continuing to gather witness statements, forensic evidence, mobile phone records, communication data, and CCTV footage to establish the full circumstances surrounding the death. Police stated they are awaiting detailed post-mortem results before proceeding with formal charges. The body was sent for autopsy to confirm the cause of death before being released to the family.

The case has drawn widespread public attention due to the young age of the suspect and the brutal nature of the crime. It has reignited debate about juvenile violence, the role of social media in intensifying jealousy and relationship conflict among teenagers, and the adequacy of existing support systems for young people experiencing emotional instability in romantic relationships. Police noted that similar cases involving teenage suspects motivated by jealousy over online communication have become increasingly common in recent years.
