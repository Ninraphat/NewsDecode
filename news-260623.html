PM Denies Weaponising DSI as Forex Scam Probe Ensnares Opposition MP; Allegations of Political Motivation Dismissed

Prime Minister Anutin Charnvirakul has dismissed allegations that the Department of Special Investigation (DSI) is being weaponised against the main opposition People's Party (PP) following a high-profile probe into illicit foreign exchange trading networks. The controversy erupted after the DSI named list-MP Pawoot Pongvitayapanu during a progress report on its crackdown on illegal forex investment networks, revealing that investigators had detected 14 wire transfers of two million baht each — totalling 28 million baht — flowing from a company implicated in the scheme to the MP's accounts.

The DSI, working in conjunction with the Cyber Crime Investigation Bureau (CCIB), the Central Institute of Forensic Science, and the Bank of Thailand, had earlier executed search warrants at 24 locations across Bangkok, Pathum Thani, Samut Prakan, and Samut Sakhon in an operation dubbed "Shutdown the Laundering." Authorities seized five luxury vehicles, over 65 million baht in cash, gold bars, diamond jewellery, luxury bags, watches, firearms, and four hardware wallets containing Bitcoin and USDT. More than 70 bank accounts were frozen.

The syndicate allegedly operated by masquerading as authorised brokers, luring victims with lavish displays of wealth on social media — overseas holidays, sports cars, and portfolios showing extraordinary returns. Personal sales representatives were assigned to cultivate client relationships, while early withdrawals were permitted to build credibility. Later, brokers imposed stringent conditions, levied arbitrary fees, or halted withdrawals entirely. Over 500 victims have been identified, with losses exceeding 70 million baht in just one segment of the network.

To evade Thai legal jurisdiction, most brokers registered their operations offshore in locations such as Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, the Seychelles, the Cayman Islands, and the Comoros. The scheme violated multiple laws including the Emergency Decree on Borrowings, the Securities and Exchange Act, the Derivatives Act, and the Computer-Related Crime Act, as well as public fraud provisions under Section 343 of the Penal Code.

The PP leadership acknowledged the matter publicly, stating that Pawoot is prepared to cooperate with authorities and remains only a person of interest — not yet a formal suspect. However, the party raised concerns about the timing of the disclosure, noting that Pawoot had recently intensified scrutiny of the government's TH-AI Passport project. Mr Anutin flatly denied any retaliatory intent: "If I were trying to politically harass people, many others would have already been targeted," he said.
