Myanmar Suspect Arrested in Yangon After Dismembering Wife and Concealing Remains in Bangkok Hotel Room; Jealousy Over New Partner Cited as Motive

A Myanmar national has been apprehended in Yangon and is being extradited to Thailand after allegedly murdering and dismembering his 34-year-old wife, Aye Aye Nwe, a bar waitress who worked in the Klong Toey area of Bangkok. Her dismembered remains were discovered stuffed inside a plastic storage box left on the rear balcony of a rented room on Ratchaprarop Soi 14 in Ratchathewi district shortly after midnight on Friday.

Police identified the victim's husband as the prime suspect after reviewing security camera footage that showed him leaving the scene and subsequently hiring a passenger van to travel from Bangkok to Mae Sot district in Tak province, in an apparent attempt to flee across the border into Myanmar. Investigators believe the killing stemmed from jealousy after the suspect accused his wife of having a new romantic partner.

Pol Col Sakkaya Saengwan, deputy commander of Metropolitan Police Division 1, confirmed he would travel to Mae Sot to take custody of the suspect at the immigration checkpoint before transferring him back to Bangkok for legal proceedings. Thai police had coordinated with Myanmar authorities and Interpol to locate and detain the suspect before he could move further into Myanmar.

CCTV footage from the building and surrounding streets proved decisive in reconstructing the suspect's movements and establishing a clear timeline of events. Officers from the forensic unit secured the crime scene and the storage box was transported for a full autopsy at the Institute of Forensic Medicine in Bangkok. Forensic investigators also collected DNA samples and fingerprint evidence from the rented room.

The case has drawn significant public attention due to the brutal nature of the crime and the speed with which police were able to trace the suspect across the border. Authorities said the case underlines the importance of regional cooperation between Thai and Myanmar law enforcement in apprehending fugitives who attempt to exploit border crossings as escape routes following violent crimes. The suspect faces charges of premeditated murder and concealment of a corpse upon his return to Thailand.
